Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

Who can come?

Chosen is built for young Catholics aged 16–28 across Australia. You're welcome whether you're coming with a parish group, a youth ministry, a few friends, or on your own — and whether you're brand new to the faith or have been walking with the Church for years.

How much does it cost?

Pricing tiers and registration windows will be confirmed before the registration link goes live. Expect early-bird, standard, and group-of-ten rates. We'll never let cost be the reason someone misses Chosen — reach out to your local Jesus Youth chapter if you'd like to talk about a scholarship.

Where will I stay?

On-campus accommodation at La Trobe University is available as part of registration. We'll also provide options for off-campus stays nearby for those bringing larger groups or who prefer to organise their own.

How do I get there?

La Trobe University, Bundoora, is about 35 minutes north-east of Melbourne CBD by public transport. Closest tram stop is on the 86 line; closest train station is Macleod (Hurstbridge line). Most interstate attendees fly to Melbourne (MEL) and travel to campus from there. We'll publish detailed travel notes closer to the date.

What's your child-safeguarding policy?

Jesus Youth Australia takes child safeguarding seriously. All adult volunteers and team members hold current Working with Children Checks and complete safeguarding training. We follow the Catholic Church's National Catholic Safeguarding Standards. Read our full Child Safety page for details, and please contact us if you have any concerns.

Can my parish send a group?

Yes — and we'd love to host you. Group registrations of ten or more receive a discounted rate, and we'll help coordinate accommodation and transport. Get in touch via the registration page or your local Jesus Youth chapter to start planning.
