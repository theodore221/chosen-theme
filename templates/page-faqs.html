Who is CHOSEN for?

Young people aged 16 to 28. That is the heart of our audience — university students, young professionals, late-stage high-schoolers, and everyone in between. If you sit just outside that range and feel called to come, write to us.

Do I need to be Catholic to attend?

No. CHOSEN is a Catholic conference shaped by Catholic worship and teaching, but anyone genuinely curious is welcome. Bring non-Catholic friends. Many people have first encountered Christ through a friend who simply said yes to inviting them.

I have never been to a conference like this. Will I feel out of place?

Most people are first-timers. You do not need to know the prayers, the songs, or the right things to say. Come as you are — confused, curious, hesitant, hopeful. The community is warm, the rhythm is gentle, and no one is keeping score.

Is there a dress code?

Dress modestly and comfortably — you will be on your feet, in chapel, in workshops, and in worship. Bring something a little more reverent for Mass and adoration if you can. Specific guidelines TBC closer to the event.

Are meals included? Can you cater for dietary needs?

Where will I sleep?

On-campus accommodation at La Trobe University is being arranged for delegates who want it. You can also book your own hotel or Airbnb in the surrounding suburbs of Bundoora, Macleod, or Greensborough. Pricing and booking details TBC.

Can I come for just one day?

CHOSEN is designed as a four-day journey — the rhythm builds across the days and the fruit comes from staying in it. Day passes may be available for some sessions. We strongly encourage the full experience. TBC.

Can my parish or youth group come together?

Yes, and we love it when groups travel together. Group registration support is available — write to us and we will help with bulk bookings, group accommodation, and connecting your leader with our team.

Is CHOSEN safe for under-18s? What about safeguarding?

Absolutely. Jesus Youth Australia adheres to the Catholic Church's National Catholic Safeguarding Standards, and every volunteer working with minors holds a current Working With Children Check. Under-18 delegates have additional supervision and clear safeguarding protocols throughout.
